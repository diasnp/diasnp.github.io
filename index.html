Problem

What we aim to target.

Diabetes is considered as a major global health problem. Type 2 diabetes (T2D) is a chronic disease characterized by decreased insulin secretion and increased insulin resistance, accounting for more than 90% diabetes cases.
Although many anti-diabetic drugs and guidelines are availble, almost 50% of diabetic patients fail to reach their treatment goal. Also, the glucose-lowering response to anti-diabetic drug differs significantly between individuals.
Physicians need help in choosing the right drug for the right patient at the right time.
Type 2 diabetes (T2D) in all cases 90%
T2D patients failed to have drug responses 50%
Genetic variations' contribution of responses to the first-line drug Metformin 34%
